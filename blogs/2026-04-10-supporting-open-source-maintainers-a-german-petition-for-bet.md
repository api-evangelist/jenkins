<div class="paragraph">
<p>Open source software, as Jenkins, is the foundation of modern software development.
Contributors maintain plugins, review pull requests, fix security issues, and support users, often alongside their regular jobs.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Many of these contributions happen in what is often called <strong>“Ehrenamt”</strong> (voluntary work), especially in Germany.
However, the work done by thousands of volunteers for this goal is not recognized in Germany as volunteering, neither fiscally nor in terms of funding.</p>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="a-petition-addressing-german-legislation"><a class="anchor" href="#a-petition-addressing-german-legislation">A Petition Addressing German Legislation</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>A German initiative is raising awareness for this challenge and calling for improvements in the legal and structural framework for open source contributors:</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p><a href="https://www.openpetition.de/petition/online/recognition-of-work-on-open-source-as-volunteering-in-germany">Petition: Recognition of Work on Open-Source as Volunteering in Germany</a></p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>The goal of this petition is to <strong>encourage German legislators to improve the current situation for open source maintainers</strong>.
It highlights that open source has become critical digital infrastructure, yet the people maintaining it often lack proper recognition and supportive legal conditions.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>While the initiative is focused on Germany, its message resonates far beyond national borders.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="why-this-matters-for-jenkins"><a class="anchor" href="#why-this-matters-for-jenkins">Why This Matters for Jenkins</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Jenkins is a community-driven project.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>From core maintainers to plugin authors, documentation writers, and community organizers, Jenkins exists because people contribute their time and knowledge.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>At the Jenkins Contributor Summit and many community discussions, one theme comes up repeatedly:</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p><strong>How do we make open source contribution more sustainable?</strong></p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Maintainers face increasing challenges:</p>
</div>
<div class="ulist">
<ul>
<li>
<p>Growing number of contributions and pull requests</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Rising expectations around security and quality</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Limited time and resources</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>These challenges are not unique to Jenkins, they affect the entire open source ecosystem.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="german-contributors-in-the-jenkins-community"><a class="anchor" href="#german-contributors-in-the-jenkins-community">German Contributors in the Jenkins Community</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Jenkins has a strong and active group of contributors from Germany.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>This is also reflected in the project’s leadership and governance.
With contributors like <a href="https://www.jenkins.io/blog/authors/notmyfault/">Alex Brandes</a> and <a href="https://www.jenkins.io/blog/authors/strangelookingnerd/">Daniel Krämer</a> serving on the Jenkins Governance Board, and roles such as the Events Officer <a href="https://www.jenkins.io/blog/authors/stefan_spieker/">Stefan Spieker</a> being represented from Germany, the German open source community plays an important role in shaping Jenkins.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Like many others in the ecosystem, these contributors invest significant time into maintaining and evolving Jenkins, often alongside their regular professional responsibilities.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>This makes the topic of better legal and structural support for open source maintainers especially relevant.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="a-shared-responsibility"><a class="anchor" href="#a-shared-responsibility">A Shared Responsibility</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Open source is not just a development model, it is shared infrastructure.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Companies, public institutions, and individuals all rely on projects like Jenkins.
Supporting maintainers is therefore not only a community concern, but also a topic for policymakers.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Initiatives like this petition aim to bring open source sustainability into legislative discussions and improve the framework conditions for contributors.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
