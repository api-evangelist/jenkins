</p>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>We’re introducing a fresh new look for the <a href="https://plugins.jenkins.io/credentials/">Credentials</a> plugin,
one of the most widely used Jenkins plugins and the foundation for securely storing and supplying credentials
across Jenkins.</p>
</div>
<hr />
<div class="sect2">
<h3 id="so-whats-new"><a class="anchor" href="#so-whats-new">So what’s new?</h3>
<div class="paragraph">
<p><strong>Integration with the new Manage Jenkins UI</strong></p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>This update brings the plugin into Jenkins’ experimental Manage Jenkins UI, aligning credential management with
the broader interface modernization underway in Jenkins core. Jenkins has been rolling out an experimental Manage
Jenkins layout alongside a wider redesign focused on clarity, accessibility, and reducing older navigation patterns,
and the Credentials plugin now joins that effort with its own refreshed experience.  ￼</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p><strong>Goodbye tables</strong></p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>The old table-based layout has been replaced with card-based views that are much easier to scan and navigate.
Information is presented more clearly, with less visual clutter and a stronger focus on the actions people need.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p><strong>Wizards and dialogs</strong></p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Creating credentials now happens through guided wizards that make each step clearer and easier to follow.
Actions such as editing, moving, and deleting credentials are now handled in dialogs, keeping users in context
instead of sending them through a chain of separate pages.</p>
</div>

<h3 id="available-now"><a class="anchor" href="#available-now">Available now 🚀</h3>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>The update is available now for users on Jenkins 2.541.1 and later.</p>
