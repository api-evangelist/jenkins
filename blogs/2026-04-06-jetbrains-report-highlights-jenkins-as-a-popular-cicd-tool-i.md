<div class="paragraph">
<p>A recent JetBrains blog post, <a href="https://blog.jetbrains.com/teamcity/2026/03/best-ci-tools/"><strong>"Best CI/CD Tools for 2026: What the Data Actually Shows"</strong></a>, provides an overview of CI/CD tool adoption and trends.
According to the report, Jenkins continues to play a major role in the ecosystem, with a <strong>28% adoption rate</strong>, ranking just behind GitHub Actions.
This highlights that Jenkins remains a widely used and trusted solution across organizations of all sizes.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>The article highlights that while newer tools focus on ease of use, Jenkins’ enduring value lies in its unparalleled <strong>autonomy and extensibility</strong>.
Here are the key strengths of Jenkins as identified in the report:</p>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="1-flexibility-and-control"><a class="anchor" href="#1-flexibility-and-control">1. Flexibility and Control</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>The blog emphasizes that Jenkins is the premier choice for teams that need "full control over infrastructure and data."
Unlike SaaS-based CI tools, Jenkins allows organizations to own their entire environment, which is a critical requirement for:</p>
</div>
<div class="ulist">
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Strict Security &amp; Compliance:</strong> Organizations with sensitive data that cannot leave private infrastructure.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Self-Hosting:</strong> Teams that prefer to manage their own compute resources to avoid the "black box" nature of cloud-native CI.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="2-a-strong-and-open-ecosystem"><a class="anchor" href="#2-a-strong-and-open-ecosystem">2. A Strong and Open Ecosystem</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Even in 2026, Jenkins' ecosystem remains its greatest asset.
The report highlights that:</p>
</div>
<div class="ulist">
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Universal Integration:</strong> It can integrate with almost any tool in the software development lifecycle, from legacy version control systems to modern cloud providers.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Custom Workflows:</strong> Because of its open-source nature and thousands of plugins, it supports highly specialized or "bespoke" workflows that standard commercial tools might not accommodate.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="3-resilience-in-complex-and-legacy-environments"><a class="anchor" href="#3-resilience-in-complex-and-legacy-environments">3. Resilience in "Complex and Legacy" Environments</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>The article categorizes Jenkins as the "Best fit" for <strong>complex or legacy setups.</strong>
While newer tools excel at "greenfield" projects (starting from scratch), Jenkins is praised for its ability to:</p>
</div>
<div class="ulist">
<ul>
<li>
<p>Manage "long-lived" setups that have evolved over years.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Support diverse, multi-platform environments (Windows, Linux, macOS).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Handle "heavy" pipelines that require professional DevOps expertise to orchestrate.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="4-open-source-and-cost-transparency"><a class="anchor" href="#4-open-source-and-cost-transparency">4. Open Source and Cost Transparency</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>As an open-source tool, Jenkins offers a significant financial advantage: <strong>no licensing fees.</strong>
For large-scale enterprises with the engineering talent to maintain it, Jenkins provides a way to scale without the per-user or per-minute costs associated with commercial platforms like TeamCity or CircleCI.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="5-mature-pipeline-as-code"><a class="anchor" href="#5-mature-pipeline-as-code">5. Mature Pipeline-as-Code</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>The blog acknowledges Jenkins’ commitment to modern practices through the <code>Jenkinsfile</code>.
This allows teams to define build, test, and deployment logic as code, ensuring that even this "venerable" tool remains compatible with GitOps and modern version-control-driven development.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="a-balanced-perspective"><a class="anchor" href="#a-balanced-perspective">A Balanced Perspective</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>The JetBrains report also notes that Jenkins typically requires more operational effort compared to fully managed CI/CD services.
This is an important consideration: Jenkins provides flexibility and control, but it also assumes that teams are willing to invest in operating and maintaining their setup.
For many organizations, this trade-off is intentional.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="sect1">
<h2 id="summary"><a class="anchor" href="#summary">Summary</h2>
<div class="sectionbody">
<div class="paragraph">
<p>The report reinforces what many in the community already know:
Jenkins continues to be a <strong>reliable and flexible CI/CD platform</strong> that adapts to a wide variety of use cases.</p>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>Its strength lies not in being the simplest tool, but in being one of the most <strong>adaptable and extensible</strong>.
As the CI/CD landscape evolves, Jenkins remains a strong choice for teams that value:</p>
</div>
<div class="ulist">
<ul>
<li>
<p>Control over their infrastructure</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Flexibility in their workflows</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A mature and active open source ecosystem</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="paragraph">
<p>And most importantly, it continues to be shaped by the contributions of its global community.</p>
</div>
